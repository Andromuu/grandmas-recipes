Potaje de Berros

Description:

The famous Potaje de Berros is one of the most consumed specialties in the Canary Islands. There is no Canarian home that does not have an interpretation of this appreciated recipe on its weekly menu, made mainly with fresh watercress, potatoes and white beans.

Ingredients:

  • 500 g of watercress
  • 250 g of pinto beans
  • Â½ kg of potatoes
  • 250 g salted pork meat (optional)
  • 1 pineapple millet
  • 1 onion
  • 1 tomato
  • 2 or 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 green pepper
  • saffron
  • oil
  • salt

Steps:

  1. The beans are left to soak the night before.
  2. The next day, put the beans on the fire in a cauldron with water, then add the finely chopped watercress and the pineapple to the cauldron, which is usually cut into slices, and even shelled in some cases.
  3. All this is stewed well and then we add the potatoes, cut into not very small pieces so that they do not fall apart.
  4. Along with this, the well-cut and prepared onion, pepper and tomato are poured into the cauldron.
  5. Finally, add the crushed or sliced garlic cloves, along with a splash of oil and a little saffron and salt for taste.

Did you know? Watercress is considered one of the oldest vegetables consumed by humans. Currently it has spread throughout the world as it is a highly appreciated plant for domestic consumption.
